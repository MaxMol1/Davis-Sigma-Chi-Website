Our Mission

Sigma Chi’s core values are friendship, justice and learning. Their vision is to become the preeminent collegiate leadership development organization-aligned, focused and living their core values. Their mission is to develop values-based leaders committed to the betterment of character, campus and community.

Our Chapter

The chapter house is located next to Kappa Alpha Theta, Sigma Nu and Tau Kappa Epsilon at 400 Parkway Circle directly across from the Activities and Recreation Center (ARC). As of August 2005, the National Fraternity has added the Huntsman Cancer Research Institute to it’s official list of charities. The Theta Omicron chapter will continue its work to support them through their philanthropic efforts which have averaged over $20,000 in recent years.

Puppy Love

Puppy Love is a philanthropic event that the Theta Omicron Chapter hosts every year. On March 7th from 11-3pm, puppies will be brought to the house from Hearts for Paws, a non-profit rescue organization that saves dogs throughout Yolo County from poor conditions and near euthanasia circumstances. Tickets will be $5 leading up to the event and $7 at the door, so buy your ticket from a brother or at our table at the MU! All profits will be donated to Hearts for Paws.
